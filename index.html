My Second Brain Setup with Claude Code

AI & Productivity · 2026.02.27

I set up my second brain using Claude Code. I now use Claude to perform research, create and update documents, and even reach out to people and send messages on my behalf.

I instantiated Claude in my Obsidian vault where I store all my daily notes in markdown. In the morning, I ask Claude to search all my sources (chats, posts, pull-requests, documents, etc.) and create a list with my top priorities for the day, week, and beyond (P0, P1, & P2). I then focus on my top priorities and ask Claude to remember things throughout the day or add things to my list.

I also spin up multiple sessions to research issues in our codebase or project and write design docs for improvements, while I focus on other coding tasks. At the end of the day, I ask it to summarize my accomplishments for the day and what needs to be carried over to the next.

The wildest part was having Claude read a message someone sent me in response to an inquiry. I asked them for a pointer and they told me to reach out to someone else. I asked Claude to read the message, and reach out to the other person, with a message in my writing style. I had to quickly head to a meeting, and when I sat down, I saw her name at the top of my list with a message that had been sent on my behalf — it looked as if I wrote it.

One Year at Meta

Reflection · 2025.02.05

Today marks 1 year since I joined Meta. I've really enjoyed the opportunity to build features for the In-App Browser in Facebook and Instagram for iOS, working with great people to deliver impact together. Here are the top 4 things I learned over the past year.

Focus on Impact. The focus on impact at Meta prompted me to be thoughtful of the results of my work. You could write 1000 lines of code for something with little impact, or you could write 10 lines of code on something with a lot of impact.

Data-Driven Decisions. When suggesting ideas for new features, I found that the ideas with data supporting their value were more likely to move forward.

Share your Work. I found it valuable to communicate updates on my projects to a broader audience through Workplace. Building visibility around your work matters.

Network is Important. I've learned a lot from getting to know people in my org and the company — whether by reaching out to a completely different team to learn how they solved a problem I'm working on, or by grabbing lunch with members of other teams. Building relationships is crucial for driving innovation and unlocking opportunities.

This journey is only 1% finished. Looking forward to another year of learning and building awesome things.
