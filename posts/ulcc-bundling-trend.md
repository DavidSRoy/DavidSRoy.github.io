---
title: Ultra Low-Cost Carriers Are Embracing Bundles
date: 2025-09-04
category: Aviation
---

I'm noticing a new trend where ultra low-cost carriers (ULCCs) that have historically provided "unbundled" fares are now offering mini bundles that package multiple services together.

Earlier this year, while booking a ticket with VivaAerobus, a Mexican ULCC, I noticed something unexpected. Historically, ULCCs have operated on the premise that you just pay for the seat, and everything else comes with an additional fee. However, during this booking process, I was presented with 3 bundle options: Zero (the most basic fare with just a seat and a personal item), Light (includes a carry-on), and Smart, the most premium option which comes with what you would expect on a legacy carrier, plus priority boarding. The options were structured in a way where it was cheaper to choose the bundle rather than select the individual services separately.

In a similar approach, Spirit and Frontier have recently revamped their business models by introducing bundled fares. Travelers are now presented with 4 options, from most basic to most premium.

I predict this will resonate well with travelers who generally don't enjoy traveling on ULCCs, especially because of ancillary fees for what might seem like basic services (e.g. bringing a carry-on). Additionally, reducing their offerings to just 4 bundles will make it easier for customers to choose what works for them. ULCCs like Spirit and Frontier have been struggling recently due to the increased demand for premium and long-haul travel. Let's hope these changes lead to an improved passenger experience and better performance for these carriers.
